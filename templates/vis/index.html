Data Sources

The data is a compile data table comes from many sources. Most of the data comes from the ONS (Office for National Statistics). However, any data provided that is not available from the ONS was taken from these Sources:
Tax Rates: Institute For Fiscal Studies
Pre-1900s Data: Uk Public Spending
GDP 1840-2014: Measuring Growth

     Welcome to the Economic Visualisation Application!
    An Application for the economic analysis of Historic data and its comparison to economic theory and their models.
Best performance is achieved using Chrome (around 3x as fast). It is recommended to use Chrome!
If you don't know how anything works look at the help page or hover over the item to get more information in the tooltip
Left Control - This area allows to change the properties of the visualisations. From activating a Time Range Bar to zoom in the graph, to background colour selection.
Visualisation Area - This area is split into the Main, Historic Visualisation of UK economic properties over the past 300 years. Then you get the upper visualisations that show the relation between economic theory and their models (blue curves), with the historic data from the Historic graph (green dots).
Series Selection - You can select or deselect which series (economic property) you want by either clicking on the label of it; toggling that series, or double clicking which selects only that value or selects all values.
Right Control - This shows a list of data combinations that will limit the series that are shown by the main (historic) visualisation. Hover over the buttons to see details about each category.
For extra info you can scroll down.
