Upper Visualisations

Phillips Curve


Axis: Y: Inflation %, The Consumer Price Index (inflation). X: Unemployment % of Population
This displays the Phillips Curve in blue along with the historic data of the Phillips curve in green. The visualisation clearly displays that the theoretic assumption of the Phillips curve is true. The blue curve shows the theoretical curvature and values of the Phillips curve. The green points show the points that the curve took in practice. This means with the specific inflation % and unemployment rates. The highlighted points show the points that is selected/focused in the main visualisation.

Laffer Curve


Axis: Y: Tax Revenue as % of GDP, X: Income Tax Rate %
This displays the Laffer Curve in blue along with the historic data of the Laffer curve in green. The actual tax rates were never too high or too low therefore, it is difficult to say if the Laffer curve is true in practice. The blue curve shows the theoretical curvature and values of the Laffer curve. The green points show the points that the curve took in practice. This means with the specific inflation % and unemployment rates. The highlighted points show the points that is selected/focused in the main visualisation.

IS-LM Model


Axis: Y: Central Bank Interest Rate %, X: Real GDP in billions
This displays the IS-LM Model, also called the Hicks–Hansen model in blue along with the historic data of the IS-LM Model Curve in green. IS is the "investment–saving" curve that shows a inverse linear relationship between interest rates and real economic output (GDP).
LM is the "liquidity preference–money supply" upward-sloping curve representing the role of finance and money in the economy. liquidity preference means the demand for money and money supply is how much money is actually available. The LM curve is upward sloping because higher income results in higher demand for money, thus resulting in higher interest rates. The intersection of the IS curve with the LM curve shows the equilibrium interest rate and price level. As can be seen the historic data follow the IS curve (downwards sloping), however not so much for the LM curve(upwards sloping) The blue curve shows the theoretical curvature and values of the IS-LM Model Curve. The green points show the points that the curve took in practice. This means with the specific inflation % and unemployment rates. The highlighted points show the points that is selected/focused in the main visualisation.

How to use the Application

The application is meant to display economic data about the United Kingdom.

Series Selection


This allows for a textual selection of the series that you want to be displayed. The keyword wanted should be typed in, for example "Tax". Once, you typed in the keywords, you click "Go!". This will selected the desired series as specified in the search box.

Many keywords can be entered just add ", " between each of them and all the series with any of those keywords will be displayed. For example: "%, GDP, Tax, Population". All can be selected using "All"

Data Combinations


This panel, below the visualisations shows a list of interesting data and series combinations. For example, "Spending Vs. Public Debt" shows the Government spending and Public Debt series. This shows the correlation between the two and economic conclusions can be made from them.

Main Visualisation Controls


This panel controls the Main visualisation properties. There are 3 properties that can be changes:
  1. "Interactive Mode" - This toggles if the tooltip style. Either its tooltip displaying 1 point, or the tooltip shows all series in that particular year.
  2. "Navigation Chart" - This toggles the Navigation chart that allows for the x axis to be shortened or extended.
  3. "Normalise" - This Normalises the data. This means that all the data curves will be the same height. All data will be between 0 and 1.

Main Visualisation Legend


This panel is above the main visualisations and displayes all the series information: The Series name and The Series Colour. If the circle to the left of each series is full then that means the series is being displayed, Otherwise, the circle is hollow. This means that the series is disabled and not displayed.
The legend is also a control tool. It can enable or disable series. There are two actions that can be performed:
  • Single Click - This toggles the series that is being clicked. This means it enables the series if disabled, or disables the series if enabled.
  • Double Click - This disables all other series, or displays all series. If many series are enables and a double click is performed, that series is shown only, all others are disabled. If only that one is enabled, the double click will enable all series.
